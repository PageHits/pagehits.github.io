How to calibrate your computer screen

When dealing with creative design, where color accuracy is crucial, ensure you're on a computer with a calibrated screen.

Screen cali… what?

"Calibrating" your monitor is a fancy term that simply means adjusting its colors to match a common standard.

It is stupid easy. And takes less time than your average coffee break. Most importantly, you don't need to buy fancy equipments or download specialized softwares. Because the color calibration utility comes

Okay, that was not completely correct. Professionals often use devices called spectrophotometers or colorimeters that hangs off the screen and works with software for studio-grade color accuracy. But then if you're savvy enough to own a spectrophotometer, you already know how it works. This article is for folks who don't have a design background, but need to manage or collaborate with designers on a regular basis.

Why calibrate?

  • A calibrated screen ensures you're viewing creatives the same way the creator intended it to.

  • It also ensures your users, on an average, views your creatives the way you want them to. Of course you have no control on whether your end-users' devices are calibrated or not. But if you yourself are on a calibrated screen, it limits the variation to a great extent.

How to calibrate?

Thankfully, both Windows and Mac come with built-in calibration tools that guide you

On a Mac

Typically Apple devices come pre-calibrated from the factory. So in most cases, you pretty much don't have to do anything else. However, if you're connecting to an external display or some other 3rd party software seems to have shifted the colors on your main display, and you suspect the screen is looking funny all of a sudden, here are the steps to fix it.

Refer to this MacOS User guide article for the most up-to-date info, as they keep changing slightly between versions.

  1. Bring up System Preferences, then click Displays .
  2. Click the Color Profile pop-up menu, then choose Customize. A list of color profiles for your display and other color profiles appears.
  3. Click the Add button to open Display Calibrator Assistant.

Display Calibrator Assistant walks you through adjusting your display, then creates a calibrated color profile. The number of adjustments in the calibration process varies depending on the type of display.

When the display profile is ready, it’s automatically assigned as your display’s color profile.
