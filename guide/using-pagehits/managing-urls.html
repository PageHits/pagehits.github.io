Managing URLs

Setting your URL

If you've been following the previous chapter where we explain how to build pages, you must have designed your first landing page by now. Excellent!

Now, for the rest of the world to be able to view it, you need to first publish it by assigning it an address. This address is also known as the URL - this is what your someone needs to type on their browser's address bar to get to your page.

Choosing URL

When it comes to choosing your URL, you have got two options.

  1. Your own domain (Recommended)

    If you own a domain, you can link it with your PageHits landing pages. For example, if you own awesome.com, your can make your address look like www.awesome.com/limited-time-offer.

  2. Use a free domain

    If you don't own a domain, PageHits provides the domain on-pagehits.com to you free of cost. And in this case, you can make your URL look like awesome.on-pagehits.com.

What if my domain is already in use?

Some of you may have your blog, website or other existing services running on your domain and you might not wish to disturb that. That's perfectly fine. You can use your domain for multiple services simultaneously. In this case, you can simply construct your landing page's URL by assigning a subdomain and adding it to the front of your domain name. Commonly used subdomains are go, try, and so on.

SSL by default

Irrespective of whether you bring your own domain or use PageHits's free domain, PageHits provides a SSL Certificate by default. Certificates are provisioned by LetsEncrypt and can take upto 24 hours to take effect.

Domains: An overview

But first, a brief primer on domains. And how they are routed over the Internet. If you are aware of this stuff already, feel free to skip over to the next section.

Breakdown of a URL

Say you're a vigilante billionaire. And your website address is https://www.killthejoker.com. This is what your visitors have to type in their browser's address bar to reach your site.

Let's break down this URL to see what is it made up of, in reverse order:

  • The domain - killthejoker.com. A domain is a name that you own and must have purchased from a Domain registrar, such as GoDaddy, Namecheap, Google Domains, etc.
  • The subdomain - www The piece of text that goes in front of your domain is totally upto you. Most companies leave it as www, but it does not need to be. It is also optional, and can be omitted.
  • and the schema - http or as in this case, the more secure https

Purchase your domain

Go ahead and buy your domain, if you haven't already. PageHits does not recommend any particular Domain registrar over the rest. Just use your best judgement and do your research and pick one that fits your budget, ease of use and support requirements. You can always switch registrars while retaining your domain - so no need to sweat over this.

Mapping a domain to multiple services

When you buy a domain, it usually comes bundled with a web-hosting plan. And your domain is pre-configured to point to that hosting service. So that your website is live right out of the box.
